## The Fey Crossing and Its Guardian

The crossing he emerged near was ancient and dangerous. It lay where stone, dream, and moonlight intersected, usable only when a **blue moon** aligned the Feywild and Material Plane just enough to blur their reflections.

The guardian was a fey bound not to the crossing itself, but to its **secrecy**.

Their role was not to bar passage, but to ensure that:

* The crossing could not be found by accident
* Its presence was masked by a **false aura**, making it feel mundane, inert, or cursed depending on who approached
* Only those dreaming correctly could perceive it as a door

The ritual to maintain this deception required moonlight that did not belong, hence the blue moon. The guardian wove illusion, memory, and misdirection into a living veil renewed only once every few decades.

## Why the Giant Could See What Others Couldn‚Äôt

Stone giants dream in **stone logic**, not mortal logic.

When the giant arrived, he did not see a crossing. He saw a *possibility in the rock*, like a half-finished statue waiting for hands. Where others would have felt nothing, he felt incompleteness.

The guardian noticed this immediately.

To the Fey, anyone who perceives the lie without being told of it is either dangerous or sacred. Often both.

Rather than drive him away, the guardian challenged him with riddles that only made sense in dreams. The giant answered by carving, reshaping the stone around the crossing in ways that reinforced the false aura while strengthening the door itself.

That cooperation is the hinge point.

## The Marriage, Fey-Style

Fey marriages are not contracts of love. They are **alignments of purpose**.

By aiding the guardian, the giant bound his dreaming essence to the crossing. By choosing to stay, even temporarily, he became part of the ritual architecture. The Feywild recognized the symmetry and offered a bond.

Their union did several things:

* Anchored the crossing more deeply to the Material Plane without revealing it
* Gave the guardian access to stone giant dreamways
* Gave the giant safe passage between waking, dreaming, and Fey paths

The marriage vow likely included conditions such as:

* The giant must never name the crossing aloud under a full moon
* The guardian must never walk beneath open sky during a blue moon

## What the Ritual Was *Before* the Giant

The **Blue Moon Ritual** predates the stone giant by centuries.

Previous guardians performed it alone, using moonlight, misdirection, and layered Fey lies to project the **false aura** around the crossing. It worked. It always worked. The crossing was hidden, stable, and properly forgettable.

What the ritual *did not* do was make the crossing comfortable.

Each guardian endured it as a duty. The ritual thinned the veil but left the world abrasive, like walking barefoot on unfinished stone. Guardians rotated. Time passed. Sanity frayed, but not catastrophically.

The system was imperfect, but sustainable.

## What the Giant Changed (Without Meaning To)

When the stone giant arrived, he was not meant to be part of the ritual.

But stone giants do not dream lightly.

During one Blue Moon, at the guardian‚Äôs invitation or perhaps out of curiosity, the giant entered a trance nearby. He did not reshape the ritual or replace any component. He merely **dreamed of the crossing while it was being hidden**.

That single act mattered.

Where the ritual traditionally *overlaid* a false aura, the giant‚Äôs dream **pressed the lie into the stone itself**. Not stronger. Deeper. More convincing. The crossing didn‚Äôt just feel unimportant. It felt *unfinished*, *irrelevant*, *not yet real*.

The guardian noticed the difference immediately.

The ritual did not require the giant.
But with him present, it became **smoother**, less painful, almost‚Ä¶ intoxicating.

## The Side Effect No One Anticipated

The giant, however, felt something new.

Stone giants already believe the waking world is a poor draft of reality. During that ritual, the giant experienced something dangerously validating: a moment where **his dream reshaped truth without resistance**.

For a brief time, the world felt honest.

This was not power in the mortal sense. It was alignment. A sensation that things were finally sitting where they belonged.

When the blue moon passed, the feeling faded.

And that was the first wound.

---

## Why He Returned to It

He told himself he was helping. And he was, at first.

He participated in the ritual again, and again. Perhaps only during blue moons at first. The guardian never asked him to stop. Fey rarely interrupt someone who is ruining themselves so efficiently.

Each time, the dream-essence clung to him longer.

He began to:

* Sleep longer, waking reluctantly
* Find the waking world increasingly crude
* Hear the stone whisper fragments of unfinished dreams

Between rituals, the absence felt wrong. The world felt loud, heavy, untrue.

This is where addiction begins, Fey-style.

## The Escalation

Over years, the giant started chasing the feeling.

Not always through the ritual. Sometimes by lingering near the crossing. Sometimes by entering dream trances alone. Sometimes by following Fey paths deeper and staying longer than intended.

Each visit extended the time his mind spent in **dream-logic**, where consequences are symbolic and identity is flexible.

Physiologically, something changed.

Stone giants are creatures of memory and patience. Their bodies already respond to belief. As the giant‚Äôs mind began to accept that reality was provisional, his body followed.

Without dream-essence:

* He became withdrawn, irritable, hollow
* Stone felt dull and heavy
* The world seemed aggressively unfinished

With dream-essence:

* He dissociated
* Time lost coherence
* Objects felt *important* in ways he could not articulate

This is the pivot into the **Mad Wanderer**.

---

## The Madness, Explained

The madness is not lunacy. It is **ontological drift**.

The giant no longer fully believes the waking world is real, but he is still physically bound to it. That tension leaks outward.

Creatures near him feel:

* Reality thin
* Their actions less consequential
* Impulses sharpened and restraints softened

To him, the world resembles a half-remembered dream. In dreams, you collect what matters. Symbols. Anchors. Things that feel heavy with meaning.

So he gathers.

Animals. Relics. Broken statues. A child‚Äôs toy. A fallen tree shaped like a thought he once had.

Stone, obedient as ever, incorporates these into his body. Not armor. Not protection. **Context**.

Over time, he becomes a walking cairn of stolen significance, a landscape trying to remember itself.

## Who Is Responsible, and Who Pays the Price

* **The guardian** did nothing wrong, but also did nothing to stop it. Fey love improvements that don‚Äôt cost them anything.
* **The ritual** was never designed to be pleasurable. The giant made it so by accident.
* **The giant** chose to return, to chase the feeling, to extend visits beyond wisdom.

The cost is isolation, slow erosion of identity, and the spread of dream-madness to the world around him.

The stakes now are real:

* If he returns to the crossing again, he may anchor himself permanently to dream-state, becoming a living breach
* If he is cut off entirely, withdrawal may petrify him into an inert monument filled with trapped dreams
* If the guardian dies or the ritual fails, the giant‚Äôs accumulated dream-essence could rupture outward, revealing the crossing catastrophically
