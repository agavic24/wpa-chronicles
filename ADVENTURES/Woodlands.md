# Woodlands

*Towering redwoods and sequoias rise hundreds of feet over a bed of fallen leaves and dense greenery. Dense canopy allows only filtered dim light to reach the forest floor. To the west and southwest the trees give way to a mist-shrouded ravine; a sturdy iron-adamantine bridge crosses to the reedy wetlands, and a dark crevasse marks the entrance to the Dark Ravine. Fey crossings and ancient pacts are rumored to linger here—best tread with care.*

---

# Random Encounter Table

*Use when the party travels through the Woodlands. Roll 1d20 per travel day or at key transitions. Only three entries are combat.*

| d20 | Encounter | Type |
|-----|-----------|------|
| 1 | **Twig blights in the undergrowth.** A patch of animated twigs and thorns (use twig blight stats; 1d4+2) lurks among the roots. They strike when someone passes within 5 feet. *Combat.* | Combat |
| 2 | **Fallen giant.** A massive redwood has toppled across the path, its trunk wider than two wagons. Climbing over requires DC 12 Athletics; going around adds 20–40 minutes. Beneath the roots, a DC 13 Perception check reveals a small hollow with an old bone charm (worth 10 gp or a one-use *guidance*-like effect). | Natural Obstacle |
| 3 | **Moonlit clearing.** In a rare break in the canopy, moonlight (or filtered daylight) falls onto a ring of pale stones. The air feels still and watchful. A DC 12 Arcana or Nature check suggests this place is touched by fey or draconic magic; no portal opens unless the right ritual or key is used. Leaving an offering (silver, a song, a true name) might grant inspiration. Refusing is safe. | Non-combat / Social |
| 4 | **Lost trail.** The path vanishes under decades of leaf litter and new growth. DC 12 Survival to relocate the route; failure costs 30–60 minutes and 1d4 miles of wrong turns. No enemies. | Natural Obstacle |
| 5 | **Riddle of the roots.** Carved into the base of a sequoia are worn runes (Fey script or Draconic): *"I sleep by day and wake by night; I have no wings yet climb the height. What am I?"* (Moonlight, or a bat—DM's choice.) Speaking the correct answer causes a soft glow in a knothole; inside is a *potion of healing* or a moon-touched gem (25 gp). Wrong answers do nothing. | Puzzle |
| 6 | **Giant spiders.** 1d3+1 giant spiders have strung webs between two ancient trees. The party blunders into the web (DC 12 Perception to spot first) or the spiders descend. *Combat.* | Combat |
| 7 | **Echo of the ravine.** The ground slopes toward the west; through the trees comes the sound of wind over a great drop and the smell of thick fog. A DC 11 Survival check confirms the ravine is still distant. No encounter—just atmosphere and a reminder of the forest’s edge. | Non-combat |
| 8 | **Dryad’s request.** A dryad emerges from a venerable oak. She does not attack; she asks the party to carry a sealed acorn to “the oldest redwood they can find” or to leave a piece of metal (weapon or coin) at the base of a specific tree “to rust in peace.” Complying may earn a *goodberry* or safe passage through her grove; refusing is respected. | Social / Non-combat |
| 9 | **Slippery descent.** A steep, leaf-covered slope leads down into a hollow. DC 12 Acrobatics or Athletics to descend without falling; failure means 1d4 bludgeoning and a prone character at the bottom. Climbing back up is DC 11. | Natural Obstacle |
| 10 | **Watcher in the canopy.** Characters with passive Perception 14+ notice a flicker of movement high in the branches—something pale and serpentine, or a pair of gleaming eyes. It does not approach. If the party signals peace (no weapons drawn, a bow or wave), the presence fades. No combat; a hint of something ancient and territorial. | Non-combat |
| 11 | **Bridge of shadows.** A narrow natural bridge of roots and stone spans a shallow stream. On the far side, shadows seem to pool. Crossing is safe, but a DC 12 Investigation check reveals recent claw marks and a dropped scrap of cloth (could tie to Dark Ravine or a predator). No encounter here—clue only. | Non-combat / Clue |
| 12 | **Owlbear.** A lone owlbear (use owlbear stat block) is hunting along a game trail. It attacks if cornered or if the party gets between it and its young (nearby). Offering food or backing away slowly (DC 13 Animal Handling) can avoid combat. *Combat if triggered.* | Combat |
| 13 | **Three stones.** Three moss-covered stones stand in a triangle, each carved with a phase of the moon (waxing, full, waning). A riddle reads: *"First I grow, then I shine, then I fade—yet I return. Touch the stones in the order of my life."* Touching waxing → full → waning causes the stones to hum; a hidden compartment yields a *spell scroll* or 20 gp in silver. Wrong order does nothing. | Puzzle |
| 14 | **Fey messenger.** A sprite or pixie zips down and demands to know the party’s “business in the deep wood.” They don’t attack—they’re curious. A convincing or charming answer (DC 12 Persuasion) might yield a vague warning (“Don’t trust the dark crevice”) or directions to a safer path. Lying may earn a minor curse (e.g., disadvantage on one roll before rest) if the DM wishes. | Social / Non-combat |
| 15 | **Thick fog.** Mist rolls in from the direction of the ravine, reducing visibility to 15 feet. DC 11 Survival to keep bearing; failure adds 20 minutes of disorientation. No combat. | Natural Obstacle |
| 16 | **Abandoned shrine.** A small stone altar, overgrown with ferns, holds a cracked moon-shaped basin. No magic remains, but a DC 12 History or Religion check suggests it was once used for offerings to fey or draconic patrons. Searching reveals a tarnished silver piece (5 gp) and a faded inscription: *"The circle opens only to the key."* | Non-combat / Clue |
| 17 | **River crossing.** A swift, shallow stream cuts the path. Wading is DC 10 Athletics; failure means wet gear and 1 point of cold damage. A fallen log offers an alternative (DC 11 Acrobatics). | Natural Obstacle |
| 18 | **Bramble maze.** A wall of thorny undergrowth blocks the obvious route. DC 12 Survival to find a way through without taking 1d4 piercing damage; DC 14 to do it quietly (useful if avoiding something). Taking damage is always an option. | Natural Obstacle |
| 19 | **Sound of singing.** From somewhere ahead comes a faint, melodic voice—beautiful and slightly alien. Following leads to a clearing where the source has already gone; only trampled grass and a single luminous feather remain. The feather is non-magical but pretty (1 gp). A reminder that the wood holds more than beasts. | Non-combat |
| 20 | **Glimpse of the bridge.** Through a gap in the trees, the party sees the iron-adamantine bridge spanning the misty ravine, and beyond it the reedy wetlands. To the southwest, the darker mouth of the Dark Ravine is visible. A moment of orientation; optionally grant advantage on the next check to reach a specific landmark. | Non-combat |
