


The Duty Plaque — DM Summary
The Object
A stone duty plaque, one of several bolted sequentially around the chamber walls marking each House's term of service. House Shivarak held this post twenty years ago. When they were destroyed, their plaque was turned face-inward — a Drow gesture of House-death. Neither honored nor removed. Forgotten.
The exposed back face is rough and uncarved. Unremarkable.
But if the plaque is pulled from the wall entirely, the preserved face reveals the complete Shivarak service record — House name, sigil, dates of service, and a short list of notable passages permitted under their watch. The NPC is listed by name. Beside the entry, the sponsoring House sigil that vouched for their passage is clearly carved.

History

~20 years ago — House Shivarak holds the checkpoint post. The NPC arrives from the surface seeking passage into the Underdark. House Shivarak permits entry, sponsored by a still-existing Drow House within Szinth'alyra — likely in exchange for surface-world knowledge, goods, or political favor.
Shortly after — House Shivarak is destroyed by a rival. Their plaque is turned. Their records are considered erased.
Present day — A new House holds the post. One or two rotations have occurred since Shivarak. The current guards know nothing of the old record and would not welcome outsiders examining it.


Encounter One — Tirrimal's Exit
Solo. Stealth-forward.
Three guards from the current House are posted here. They are bored and inattentive. Tirrimal arrives from the tunnels below and must get past them without triggering a full alert.
She has options. Inspiring Movement to slip past during a moment of inattention. Tasha's Hideous Laughter if things go sideways. The plaque wall is visible to her as she passes — the face-turned Shivarak plaque is noticeable, distinct from the others. She may not understand its significance yet, but she sees it.
This plants the seed for the party's return without Tirrimal needing to know why it matters.

A Drow Sergeant and a Rotating Squad of Conscripts
A low-ranking but ambitious Drow sergeant — someone with enough skill to be useful but not enough political connections to avoid a miserable posting — commands a small rotating squad of four to six conscripts drawn from minor Houses. The sergeant has been here long enough to know every tunnel, every sound, and every smell that means trouble is coming. They operate a surprisingly organised checkpoint. Drow with House credentials pass freely. Merchants and slavers of allied races — Duergar traders, Quaggoth handlers, the occasional licensed surface smuggler — are logged, taxed a small tribute, and waved through. Anything coming down from the surface uninvited is captured if it looks valuable and killed if it doesn't. The sergeant keeps meticulous records on a thin sheet of treated leather, partly out of professional pride and partly as political insurance — knowing who passes through this intersection and when is the kind of information that has value in Drow society. If the party somehow gets hold of that ledger, it is essentially a map of Underdark politics.

1. A Drider and Its Bound Thralls
A disgraced Drow warrior transformed into a Drider makes a perfect permanent posting — it's a punishment and a practicality rolled into one. The Drider is too monstrous to participate in Drow society anymore but too powerful and too proud to simply be killed. It holds the intersection with two or three enthralled humanoids, likely captured surface dwellers conditioned through prolonged exposure to Drow enchantments. The Drider lets through anyone bearing the correct House sigil or speaking a rotating passphrase, and it lets out anyone being actively escorted by Drow soldiers from below. Everything else — wandering monsters, uninvited travellers, escaped slaves heading for the surface — it kills or recaptures with cold efficiency. The interesting wrinkle is that the Drider quietly resents its posting and its masters, which a clever party might exploit. It won't betray the Drow outright, but it might hesitate just long enough to hear an interesting offer.

## Twenty Years Ago — The Sergeant's Post

A Drow sergeant named **Vael'uss** of **House Shivarak** ran the intersection checkpoint with quiet competence and cold professionalism for nearly three decades. He maintained the leather ledger, taxed the traders, logged the travellers, and kept the tunnel approaches clean. He was good at the job — methodical, observant, politically careful — which is precisely why he was eventually *promoted* out of it. A sergeant who knows every face, every House sigil, and every piece of cargo that passes through the only surface entrance in the region accumulates exactly the kind of quiet leverage that makes him valuable at a higher level of Drow society. House Shivarak eventually recalled him to the city, rewarding his service with a position of greater standing.

When the first NPC arrived twenty years ago with genuine intent to trade and study, Vael'uss was the one who made the decision to let them pass. It wasn't generosity — it was pragmatism. A surface dweller with coin, clear purpose, and no apparent military backing was a logged curiosity and a taxable asset. Vael'uss recorded the entry in his ledger, charged the appropriate toll, and may have even provided the first NPC with basic guidance on which tunnels to avoid. Over the weeks or months that followed — as the first NPC passed back and forth through the checkpoint conducting their business — a cold professional relationship developed. Not friendship by any surface standard, but the particular Drow version of respect that exists between two capable people who find each other reliably useful. When Vael'uss was eventually recalled to the city, the first NPC was already deep in the Underdark. He left no forwarding instructions for whoever came next. Why would he? The surface dweller's business was their own problem now.

Vael'uss currently holds a position within House Shivarak's intelligence or administrative structure in the Drow city — the exact role depending on how you want to use him later. He is the only person in the Underdark who knows the first NPC by name, by face, and by the stated purpose of their original visit. Whether he considers that relationship an asset or a liability after twenty years of silence is an open and interesting question.

## The Replacement — The Drider Takes the Post

When Vael'uss departed, House Shivarak needed a replacement that required no salary, no rotation, no political management, and no ambition. They assigned **Xorryn** — a Drider, once a warrior of middling rank within the House, transformed as punishment for a failure that the House considers too embarrassing to specify and Xorryn considers a profound injustice. The posting is simultaneously practical and punitive. Xorryn is powerful enough to hold the intersection against most threats, monstrous enough that no one from another House will complain about the appointment, and trapped enough — socially, physically, psychologically — that there is nowhere else to go.

Xorryn has held the post for nearly twenty years. He has no thralls of his own and relies instead on whatever conscripts House Shivarak sends up on rotating duty — young soldiers on punishment postings of their own, none staying long enough to learn the tunnels properly or earn Xorryn's marginal trust. He despises the arrangement and despises the conscripts more. He does the job because the job is all he has left, and he does it with the particular ferocity of someone whose pride has been compressed into pure function.

Xorryn never met the first NPC. The ledger Vael'uss kept was taken back to the city with him — it was *his* ledger, his intelligence asset, not House property in his view. Xorryn was given nothing. No records, no briefing, no list of approved travellers. What he was given was a single instruction — *allow passage to anyone bearing a House Shivarak sigil or the sigil of an allied House, and kill or recapture everything else.* He has followed that instruction with mechanical consistency for two decades.

He knows Vael'uss by name and by House. He knows that his predecessor came from House Shivarak because that is the House that owns this tunnel and owns his posting. He resents Vael'uss the way he resents everything above him — which is to say completely, and with the particular bitterness of someone who suspects the other person has entirely forgotten they exist. If the party's conversation turns to the history of the post, or if they present credentials or ask about previous arrangements, Xorryn will snarl something revealing without entirely meaning to. *"Do you carry a sigil for House Shivarak? Vael'uss of that House ran this post before me. If you had business here twenty years ago, you deal with me now. He is not coming back."* It is not helpful information offered willingly. It is resentment leaking through professionalism.

## Last Week — The Second NPC's Exit

The second NPC arrived at the intersection heading *upward* — toward the surface — alone and moving with clear purpose. Xorryn moved immediately to block the passage. Whatever conscripts were on rotation that week formed up behind him. The exchange was brief.

**Tasha's Hideous Laughter** hit the conscripts first — four soldiers suddenly incapacitated, rolling on the tunnel floor in helpless hysteria, their weapons useless. Xorryn, with his considerable will, likely resisted or shook it off, but the tactical picture collapsed around him instantly. **Heat Metal** on Xorryn's weapon or the chains he carries for recapturing escapees made him recoil in genuine pain — a sensation Driders, despite their resilience, are not accustomed to receiving from a single surface dweller. In the moment of pain and tactical chaos, **Inspiring Movement** carried the second NPC cleanly past Xorryn's reach and into the ascending tunnel before he could recover and pursue.

Xorryn is alive. He is furious in the cold, focused way that indicates genuine danger rather than mere temper. The conscripts recovered after the minute was up, shaken and humiliated. Xorryn has said nothing to House Shivarak about the incident because reporting it means admitting it happened. The scorch marks on the tunnel wall near the intersection are fresh. Any character with survival instincts will clock them immediately and understand that something violent and recent occurred here.

## The Party's Arrival

When the party descends — with the second NPC among them — Xorryn will recognise the NPC before a word is spoken. The question of whether the encounter becomes a fight, a tense negotiation, or something more nuanced depends entirely on how the party handles the first thirty seconds. Xorryn wants to restore his authority. He wants acknowledgment that he cannot simply be walked past. He may demand tribute, a formal accounting of purpose, or House credentials he knows they don't have.

The name **Vael'uss** and **House Shivarak** are in the room from the moment Xorryn opens his mouth — not as a gift, but as a grievance. For a party with no other leads on the first NPC, it is the first thread of a very long rope that descends somewhere into the dark below.
