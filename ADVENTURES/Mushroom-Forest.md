# Mushroom Forest

*Massive treelike fungal stalks rise 50 feet high, their spread caps and gills filling the canopy. Sunlight peeks between parasol-like caps; below, smaller fungi stud the undergrowth—bright colors warn of toxins. The ground is a carpet of creeping mycelium, a vast network that seeks to decompose.*

*At night the forest becomes a bioluminescent gallery of blue and green light; glowing creatures hide in the bright shadows, and stealthier things hunt among the caps.*

*There are three ways into the Mushroom Forest:
1. The Northwest trail that leads through the northern Redwoods forest and up to the Verdant Outpost (past the battle of the Apes)
2. The Southwest trail that leads through the southern Redwoods forest and down to other destinations
3. The Eastern trail that leads through the Pine forest and beyond to the mountains of Isanyas Spine.
*

*All three trails connect on the northern edge of the Great Stillwater Lake. It is here that a perceptive party may see a smaller side trail that heads down the western edge of the lake. This western trail is filled with blue-capped mushrooms and leads to Merriwig's teahouse.  This is where the party can have a long rest and some fellow travelers.*

*If the party later continues down the Eastern trail, they'll walk through red-capped mushrooms along the eastern edge of the Great Stillwater Lake.  A river flows into the lake near the southeaster corner of the lake.  If they continue to follow the trail along the river it heads back to the northeast and quickly turns into a Pine forest - this is called the Bramble Pass. A mysterious grey fog settles in the pine forest and starts at the Bramble Pass and goes northeast around the river, but not over it. The pine forest trail contines along the northern bank of the river until they find a battered wooden bridge stretching across the river to a island town in the middle of the river - this is the village of Pinewood.*

Edrik is not with the party, but they remember the following:
"It says you’ll want to start at the forest’s edge and follow the blue-capped mushrooms - not the red-capped ones! Those lead to trouble from what I wrote down.”

“After some time, you’ll come upon the Great Stillwater Lake. You can’t miss it.  Walk along the north shore until you reach the western side.”

“Now, here’s the tricky bit. Merriwig’s place is hidden by a secret entrance. It won’t be obvious. Keep an eye out for a perfect ring of mushrooms similar to the other blue-caps, but these will have white spots dotting their bright azure tops. Step inside the ring and say - ‘A door for friends is never closed’. You should see a stairwell leading down to the tea house.”

### Valeith Duskrune

A female elf likely to understand the true nature of the Swamp's corruption. Eldric knew Valeith was heading to Merriwig's tea house.  That was many years ago.

Eldric said, "She believed the path to fighting corruption was to see it, test it… understand it.  And what better teacher than the ones who live between life and decay? The great Mushroom Forests. She believed they were the key. Unlike trees, which fight to stand tall against corruption, the fungal kingdoms accept it, break it down, repurpose it. She thought that if there was a way to unmake the kind of corruption you now hold in your hands, it would be found there - where death is not the end, but the beginning of something new. But if she found her answer - I do not know.  She never returned to tell me. You are right, though. There is power in that (sporion) seed… and in those ley lines. Perhaps Vaelith will know what to do…"

Merriwig will have remembered Vaelith.  She said with him for a month or so as she explored the Mushroom Forest.  She was very curious about the fungi here and ran magical experiements on them.  She also traveled to Pinewood frequently.  One day she just announced she was leaving. She would not provide details, but he gathered she was heading back to Pinewood that day.

## Merriwig Fiddlecap Teahouse

### The property

As it turns out, Merrwig decided a hidden teahouse wasn't good business and there is now a proper building hosting his teahouse.  It is a simple building with a large common room, two bunk rooms downstairs (for 4 travlers each), and his personal quarters upstairs.

Outside there is a small stable and corral. Merrwig has a mule.

Merriwig still has the hidden mushroom house nearby, but he doesn't talk about it as it is his personal labratory.  Aillig will eventually be invited to join him there. 


### NPCs

#### Merriwig 

A male gnome and alchemist with a knack for creating "inspirational brews" (some hallucinegenic). Described by Eldric as "a fun one, that Merriwig. Loves company, loves to talk, and loves his mushrooms".

#### Pell Whistock
A a nervous tinker who came from the Mirewatch Outpost in the south west and is on his way to Pinewood.  He and Merriwig are the only two here. He is exactly the kind of man who knows everything happening on a road because he travels it constantly and talks to everyone and remembers all of it. He introduces himself twice because he's not sure you caught it the first time.

He mentions that the Valdris Logging Company set up camp a couple miles from the three-way junking in the redwood grove three weeks ago. "Clean-cut timber operation", he says, glancing at your weapons nervously. He is rattled — he passed the logging camp in early morning and the men looked like they hadn't slept. He mentions the loggers only moved there recently, relocated from a pine forest further east that they abandoned in a hurry. He doesn't know why they left, and neither do the loggers he spoke to — just that the order came down from management fast, and nobody argued.

Pell repairs and sells small mechanical curiosities — music boxes, clever latches, self-winding clocks, the kind of thing that has no practical value but that people pay surprisingly well for once they've held it in their hands. He travels a fixed circuit of villages and waystation towns on a schedule only he understands, pulling a two-wheeled cart behind a mule he calls Steadfast with complete sincerity. He is not brave. He is not adventurous. He travels because it is simply what he has always done, and the road, for all its dangers, is more familiar to him now than any room with four walls. He has been doing this for twenty-three years and has survived entirely on a combination of forgettable appearance, an instinct for leaving towns one day earlier than feels necessary, and Steadfast's inexplicable ability to sense trouble before it arrives. He's on his way to Pinewood for their annual festival.

#### Marla Brandywilk
A commoner from Pinewood.  She carries a picture of her son and a simple shortsword. She arrives at dusk looking for help.
"Help me find my missing boy! He vanished into the woodlands with his father, Iridell, yesterday and has never returned. My poor husband’s body was found mutilated along the river embankment, and I fear the worst may have happened to my son as well.  Please, does anyone know anything? Have you seen my boy?"  She holds up the portrait. Merriwig and all others have not seen him.  Pell will ask her to stay until morning and he'll go with her, but she'll insist on leaving.

"I am heading back to Pinewood village shortly to continue look for him.  If you find out any information, you can find me there. Ask around for Marla Brandywilk, I’ll not be hard to find."

### Drinks Available

- Many types of common teas from black tea to green tea to jasmine, lemon, camomile, etc.
- The Kaleidoscope. This tea creates a classic hallucinogenic mushroom trip.
- Dreamers Eye. When consumed, your eyes take on a faint bioluminescent glow. For 1 hour, you gain blindsight out to 30 feet and can see invisible creatures within that range. Additionally, you have advantage on Perception checks that rely on sight in darkness or dim light. 





# The Adventure Dialogue over Discord

*After some time, you decide it's time to leave this place... and the fallen apes.*
 
*It is midday as you start to walk south down the trail.*
 
*Soft and silently you proceed as the canopy overhead closes and the light returns to that same filtered green-gold stillness it held before any of this happened. The great trunks continue to rise on either side of you.*

*After another mile or two, the trees thin out and the the air changes. Roots give way to a creeping, carpet-soft mycelium.*

*In the distance ahead you see massive fungal stalks rise 50 feet and form a new type of canopy. As you continue forward, blue and green light winks between parasol caps.*

*You have entered the **Mushroom Forest.***

*You move forward amazed by the transformation of this terrain.* 

***Suddenly**, a shudder passes through the towering caps above you and a pale rain begins to fall.*

**SPORES!**

*Thick as mist and glittering in the strange light, spores pour down from overhead as the trail begins to disappears into a darkened haze. Your instinct screams to **run for clean air**.*

(Everyone, make a DC 12 Athletics check to hold your breath and push through the cloud.)
(Roll a Con Save for your failed athletics check)

*Your lungs burn before you even realize you've inhaled the spores. Your chest feels suddenly tight and your eyes water as the acrid spores claw for purchase.*

(You have the poisoned condition and have disadvantage on Perception and Stealth as you have uncontrollable coughing fits)

*You surge forward, stumbling blindly through the haze as spores stick to your lashes and coats like wet snow.*

**COUGH! COUGH!**

*At the edge of the storm, the air suddenly clears... blue-green light still flickers ahead, but the choking curtain falls away behind you.*

*Soon the blue-capped mushroom forest opens and the trail levels, spilling you onto the north side of a lake.*

*The water lies eerily calm. As you look across the lake you see the eastern edge transitions to shorter, red-capped mushrooms.*

***You've arrived at a junction.***

*A new dirt road from the southwest has merged with the northwestern road you've come down.  The road continues along the eastern edge of the lake.*

One two story building with a round exterior and small round windows sits before you. Smoke curls from a chimney, and the sweet, herbal scent of brewing tea carries on the moist air.*

*A mule brays from a nearby corral. A small stable is the only other building here.*
