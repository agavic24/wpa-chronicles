#Mini-Adventure: “The Scent That Shouldn’t Be”
##Act 1: The Wrong Smell on the Wind
### Goal: Draw the PC out and hook their paranoia into something real.
As your PC slips away from the outpost, their senses begin to misfire… then sharpen
- Something predatory but wrong
This is the influence of the corrupted lycans tied to the green dragon’s magic
Let the player question it at first:
 “Is this real, or am I unraveling?”
### Key Moment:
 They find physical proof
- A half-devoured animal, but not eaten cleanly
- Claw marks that don’t match natural beasts
- Maybe even a glimpse of something watching, then vanishing
### Decision Point:
Follow the scent deeper into danger
Or try to circle back toward safety (the scent follows anyway… they’re already marked)

Act 2: The Circle Tightens
Goal: Force a social confrontation under threat.
The PC is surrounded, not ambushed
2–4 corrupted lycans step out, led by a dominant werewolf figure
They are wrong: twitchy, aggressive, voices edged with something draconic
They immediately clock the PC as:
“Not prey. Not pack. Not right.”
Key Interaction:
They interrogate the PC
They don’t care about truth, only alignment of instinct
Their Demand:
“Prove it. You’re like us? Then act like it.”
Examples of “proof”:
Kill a helpless creature
Reveal their hybrid form early
Swear loyalty to the “Great Rot” (dragon influence)

Decision Point (Critical Fork):
The PC must choose how to respond:
Lean In (Deception or Survival):
 Pretend to be one of them, play along, buy time
Resist (Defiance):
 Refuse and escalate tension immediately
Outplay (Cunning Wererat Style):
 Twist their logic, redirect suspicion, or provoke infighting
Whatever they choose, the group remains hostile. This isn’t a negotiation that ends cleanly.

Act 3: Moonrise and Bloodproof
Goal: Force transformation and unleash the clash of identities.
As tension peaks… the moon rises
The corrupted lycans begin transforming violently, painfully
The PC feels it too, but their transformation is different
Sharper
More controlled… or at least more aware
Trigger:
 The alpha declares:
“Now we see what you really are.”

Climax: Werewolf vs Wererat
This isn’t just a fight. It’s a philosophy clash in claws and teeth.
What Makes It Interesting:
The werewolves fight like berserkers, fueled by corruption
The wererat fights like a survivor:
Hit-and-run
Ambush
Terrain use
Environmental Options:
Dense brush or ruined terrain for hiding
Tight spaces the wererat can exploit
Open ground where the wolves dominate

Final Decision (Character-Defining):
During or after the fight, give a sharp choice:
Kill Brutally:
 Lean into the curse, survive through savagery
Escape Cleverly:
 Prove that cunning beats ferocity
Spare One (If Possible):
 Risk everything on the idea that not all are beyond saving

Aftermath Hooks
The PC now knows: these aren’t just lycans… they’re something worse
They may carry:
A new scent that marks them to others
Knowledge of a larger corrupted pack
Doubt about where they stand in this world

Tone Thread to Keep Running
Throughout the whole adventure, reinforce:
“You left to protect your party… but out here, the question isn’t whether you’re dangerous.”
“It’s whether you’re like them.”





The barn door hangs slightly open. It doesn’t creak. It just… rests there, like it was left mid-thought.

Inside, the lantern light feels weak. Not dim—just unwilling to spread.

There’s a villager standing in the far corner, back to you.

They’re breathing.

Too evenly at first. Then not evenly at all.

Their shoulders twitch. Not like shivering—like something underneath is testing the joints.

A faint, wet sound follows each movement. Not footsteps. Not quite skin.

Something in between.

They lift one hand and brace it against the wooden beam beside them.

You hear it before you understand it:

A slow, tacky peel.

Like damp skin separating from something it doesn’t want to let go of.

They turn their head slightly.

Just enough for you to see part of their face.

Their mouth is wrong.

Closed—but not fully.

The lips don’t meet. They can’t.

There are too many teeth pressing against the shape of it.

Now the change begins.

Not outward.

Inward first.

Their body tightens.

Muscle pulls tight beneath the skin like cords being drawn too hard. Veins darken—not black, but a sickly greenish shadow, spreading in branching patterns.

Then the skin itself changes.

It doesn’t split.

It doesn’t tear.

It stretches.

Gloss creeps across it—an oily sheen, catching the lantern light in dull green highlights, like algae on still water.

You’re waiting for fur.

It never comes.

The jaw opens.

Too far.

Too easily.

The corners of the mouth crease and pull, folding instead of breaking, revealing that second row of teeth beneath the first—uneven, crowded, built for crushing rather than tearing.

A thin strand of viscous saliva hangs between them, tinted faintly green.

Its whole body is now bare.

Hairless.

Exposed.

The skin slick and shining, every movement visible beneath it.

Ribs press outward when it breathes. The spine lifts in a subtle ridge down its back. Muscles flex too clearly, like something diagrammed rather than alive.

It inhales.

Deep.

Slow.

The kind of breath that isn’t panic.

It’s assessment.

Then it moves.

Not with a leap. Not with a roar.

Just a sudden, fluid shift—low and fast.

It crosses the barn in a blink and seizes a goat by the neck.

There’s a single crunch.

No struggle. No sound after.

It lets the body drop.

Silence again.

Thick. Wet. Waiting.

It turns its head toward you.

Tilts it slightly.

Watching.

Then, almost curiously…

It tries to close its mouth.

The muscles flex.

The jaw shifts.

But it doesn’t fit anymore.

Too many teeth.

Too much structure.

So it stops trying.

Leaves its mouth slightly open.

Breathing.

A faint green sheen catching the light with every movement.

And now you understand:

There was never a wolf in this thing.

Only something that learned to wear one—and forgot how to put it back on.
