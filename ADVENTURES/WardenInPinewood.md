Five years on and you would expect more clutter, more signs of being lived in, but there are still boxes in the corner unpacked. 

Everything in the cottage is sparse and meticulous. A single towel on the wash basin. Enough dishes for a single person to eat for a week. Everything about it said she either did not want company or didn't expect to ever have company.

Elizbeth goes to her pantry and takes out a small cloth, before sitting down at her kitchen table, and unwraps it. Inside is a small pile of cured meat, homemade, hung out and dried before being taken inside. 

She takes a bite, chewing furiously as she thinks about what has to be done that day. 

It was an awkward year. Usually the festival didn't fall on a Fall Moon. At least it hadn't since she'd lived there. Everyone would be expecting her to be there, but she'd have to leave early. 

She'd have to go out today and hunt. Something big, something that could keep the Beast busy. It was always easier to control when it had food to keep it busy. 

...she'd have to bring something back for the festival. It would make her feel better about not being there. Or at least try. As she grabbed her cloak and wrapped it around her shoulders she made a mental note of it. 

She'd have to go to the market. Make sure she had bait. Maybe more arrows. Some extra blankets for tomorrow night. 

Some extra chains, just to be safe.
