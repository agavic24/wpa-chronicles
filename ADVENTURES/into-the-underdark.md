The Moon Door
The Underdark entrance is entirely mundane stone and soil by day, indistinguishable from the forest floor. But under moonlight, an ancient Drow enchantment causes both the entrance and the fog to manifest simultaneously — the fog being a byproduct of the magical energy bleeding out from the opening as it unseals. The Drow engineered it this way intentionally, using the fog to mask the sound and sight of their nighttime surface raids. The villagers believe the fog is a natural phenomenon, never connecting it to the disappearances that occasionally plague the community. On a new moon, the entrance stays sealed — and the forest falls unnervingly, completely silent.

The Physical Tells
Even in the fog, something feels wrong underfoot as the party approaches. The ground subtly slopes downward in a near-perfect circle about 30 feet across — unnatural in flat forest terrain. The trees nearest the entrance have grown away from it over decades, their trunks and branches leaning outward as if recoiling. Any ranger or druid worth their salt might notice that no animals, insects, or birdsong exist within about 50 feet of the location. Dead silence in a living forest is its own kind of alarm bell.

The Smell
The Underdark has a distinctive scent — cold stone, mineral water, ancient earth and something faintly sulphurous. Under moonlight when the entrance opens, a chill draft carries that smell upward through the fog. It cuts right through the fog's own odour, which locals describe as smelling faintly like rain on hot stone. A character with a keen nose, or a beast companion, could detect it from 20-30 feet away. Drow, who know that smell intimately, would recognise it instantly and orient toward it with purpose.

Detect Magic
The entrance radiates a dual magical signature that Detect Magic would reveal beautifully. The primary aura is Illusion — the enchantment that hides the entrance during daylight hours. Underneath that, like a heartbeat beneath skin, pulses a deep Conjuration or Transmutation aura tied to the physical unsealing of the stone. Interestingly, the fog itself registers as faintly magical in the same school as the entrance, almost like static interference — a clever player might realise the fog and the entrance share the same magical fingerprint and are connected at their source.
