# The Logging Camp

## Valdris & Sons — Temporary Field Operation, Week Three
The camp is organized and professional, which makes the fear more visible. These are experienced men who know dangerous work, and they're scared. The foreman, a practical dwarf named Brek Valdris, is honest with the party immediately — they had no idea apes were here, they've lost two men to warning charges already, and he's waiting on word from the company owner about whether to pull out.
He can't leave without authorization. That's the trap he's in.

## The Two Paths
### Help them defend:
The surviving elder ape launches a coordinated night raid within 48 hours. The party knows exactly how these apes fight now — but defending a stationary camp against creatures that own the canopy is a different problem entirely.

### Persuade them to relocate:
Brek is willing but needs cover — either a written account of the ape threat signed by the party, or someone to make the case directly to the Valdris company owner in the nearest city. He genuinely doesn't want more dead men.

## The Pine Forest Thread
When asked why they left the pine forest, Brek goes quiet for a moment. "Above my pay grade," he says, refilling his cup. "All I know is the owner sent a rider at midnight, we broke camp by dawn, and three of my best cutters refused to say what they saw on their last shift." He looks at the fire. "They still won't."
The three cutters are at the camp. They can be found, and with enough trust or persuasion they'll describe something in the pine forest that has nothing to do with animals — something that made experienced woodsmen walk off the job and not look back.
