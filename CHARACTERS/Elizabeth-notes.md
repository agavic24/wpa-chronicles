The Covenant of the New Moon
Long ago, an ancient brotherhood rose not from ambition, but necessity. They were hunters, wardens of the wild, standing between isolated villages and the terrors beyond the treeline. As werewolves multiplied, these wardens struck under the lightless sky of the new moon, culling those marked by the curse. Thus was born The Covenant of the New Moon, known simply as the New Covenant.
But the werewolves were winning. One by one, the hunters fell, or worse, rose again as the very monsters they sought to destroy. Faced with extinction or transformation, a small group of half-orc hunters chose a third path: endurance. From that turning point came their defining truth. Any afflicted faced an unyielding choice: join the Covenant and master the curse in service of the innocent, or be put down before becoming the horror they once fought. Their creed hardened with time: contain what cannot be cured, destroy what cannot be contained. A mercy, however cruel. 
The original wardens who created The Covenant were half-orcs and over generations, their rites became both sacred and more brutal; all rituals were conducted in Orcish (a mandatory language that all Wardens must learn). 
Beginning under a new moon and ending at the next full, initiates wrestled the beast within. If it was not mastered by then, it never would be. Many failed. Many died. Yet the gamble worked. The Covenant turned the tide, and for a time, lycanthropy was driven back into shadow.
Then came the corruption of the Galadrian Forest. From its festering swamps rose a new strain of lycanthrope. It spread slowly, transforming many werewolves into something unrecognizable, even for their cursed souls. These corrupted werewolves could not be reasoned with and few could be saved before the corruption took complete hold of them.  These werewolves are known as the Slickskins, a hairless, mutated form of lycan that is more ravenous and dangerous than the standard werewolf. Over decades, the savage Slickskins eclipsed the old bloodlines, while the Covenant’s numbers withered against a rising tide.
At last, a final decree was given: no new initiates. All lycans were to be destroyed unless of the ancient, pure lineage tied to an existing Covenant warden. And so the Covenant endures, fewer with each passing year, guarding a world that may soon forget the difference between hunter and beast.
Slickskins
Commoners are not aware of them outright. There are rumors of savage beasts, but no one has tied them to corrupted werewolves except The Covenant.Over the years, The Covenant has gathered enough field reports to have a pretty good understanding of them despite their very secretive and covert nature.
In humanoid form, one can usually smell them first, as they are mouth breathers with a bile-laced swamp breath (rotten eggs, sewer gas, etc).  Their gums are usually permanently stained a dusky gray and some have noticeably blackened veins. All are bald, both male and female, and many wear tattoos up and down their body.

In hybrid form, the Slickskins live up to their name. Unlike standard werewolves, they are completely hairless.  Their skin is stretched tight over pronounced muscle and bone, glistening with an oily green sheen.  Dark, black veins are clearly visible beneath the surface.  Most disturbing is their jaw, which splits unnaturally wide to reveal a second row of jagged, charcoal-dark teeth.
When found, Slickskins are not alone but traveling in packs. Like traditional werewolves there is a hierarchy with an Alpha at the top of the pack.

Elizabeth Backstory
Elizabeth should have been just another corpse left in the wake of the New Covenant’s grim decree. When Hogarth found her it was a full moon, and she was in full standard werewolf hybrid mode. An outcast from a group of Slickskins, who would not accept her as one of her own. This was her first transformation, an event that occurs when a child of two Lycans reaches puberty. She was only 13.
By the Covenant’s law, her fate was sealed. There would be no trial, no mercy. But Hogarth hesitated. How had this child of Slickskins not been afflicted with the corrupted strain? He could not say. But whether it was instinct, defiance, or something quieter seeking to answer that question, he chose not to kill her. Instead, he made a far more dangerous choice. He hid her.
In secret, he prepared her for the rites of The Covenant of the New Moon. He told no one. Not because he believed she would survive, but because he believed she wouldn’t. Better, perhaps, that she die in the ritual than at the end of a blade. When the new moon came, he began the process, fully expecting it to break her. Few adults survived and she was only a child.
But Elizabeth did not break.
She endured.
Where others lost themselves to frenzy or were torn apart by the curse, she mastered it. By the time of the full moon, she stood in control of the beast within her, something no teenage lycan had achieved in decades. Hogarth saw not defiance of the curse, but something closer to divine intervention. Or a miracle. He chose to believe the former.
So he lied.
He brought her before the Covenant not as a risk, but as blood. His daughter, long kept away with her mother, now returned after tragedy. There was suspicion, of course. Protest. But the rites do not lie, and Elizabeth had passed them. She had control. By their own laws, they could not deny her place among them.
Elizabeth was a warden in The Covenant of the New Moon.

Elizabeth in Pinewood Backstory
Pinewood sits deep in the forest, ringed by water and threaded with trade roads that swell when the harvest fair comes. By day it is work and gossip, market stalls and woodsmoke, fields carved into sunlit clearings beyond the walls. By night the old grey fog rises in the trees as it always has - longer in memory than any elder’s stories - so sensible folk stay inside and let the river and the palisades argue with the dark. Outsiders call it moody weather; locals shrug and bolt their shutters. Life here is stubborn and communal: you mend what breaks, you share what you have, and you do not pretend the wild owes you safety.
Elizabeth was sent here as a warden of the New Covenant—not to preach in the square, but to watch. Her charge is the land’s edge and the deep places beneath it: routes and rumors, things that drift up from below or slip out of forgotten tunnels, patterns in disappearances, strangers who do not belong, and any sign that what was driven into the dark is climbing toward the light again. She keeps ordinary company when she must, trades in useful silence, and treats Pinewood as a listening post as much as a home. Her mission is simple to say and hard to do: see trouble before it reaches the hearth, and be ready to answer it when it does.
Elizabeth has been working as a warden in Pinewood for 5 years. Known by the locals as an accomplished hunter, most are unaware of her true occupation. In spite of her best efforts to keep to herself, the town has regarded her as something of a fixture. She is quiet, polite, generally helpful when the opportunity presents itself, and generous with the spoils of whatever animal meat and hides she brought back from her hunts. 
Once a month Elizabeth goes to the Verdant Outpost to meet with her adoptive father Hogarth. The two have a good relationship, even if he isn’t the most outwardly affectionate person, having been a very authoritarian guardian. Still, he is very proud of how Elizabeth turned out and she knows that he is. While there she turns in her reports of any activity near Pinewood and gathers news from the Covenant.
When not attending to the various businesses of the Covenant, Elizabeth does generally enjoy being outdoors. She enjoys hunting for its own sake, enjoys swimming, hikes, bird watching, and occasionally (not very good) wood carving. She tries not to consider if any of this is a manifestation of some inner lycan part of her manifesting outwardly.

Midnight’s Bloom
Midnight’s Bloom is a mystical flower with a pinkish hue. The flower only blooms under the light of the moon while rooted and when the Grey Fog is present. Harvesting the plant while it is not in bloom prevents it from blooming in the future. The flower grows roughly an hour’s walk from the Pinewood, main gates, in the deeper recesses of the forest. It can mostly be found in small clumps of between five and ten flowers, near the base of moss-coated trees. 
Elizabeth avoids going into the Grey Fog except when her harvested Bloom Pollen is running low (down to 2 grams)
- It takes 10 grams of Bloom Pollen to coat a one-handed weapon effectively enough to harm an enemy.
- It takes 2 grams of Bloom Pollen to coat a single arrow or bolt.
- The coating lasts for 1 minute before flaking off.
The Grey Fog
The village of Pinewood is situated in a vast forest and surrounded on all sides by a babbling river. At night, the thick woodland beyond the river is  covered in an eerie, grey fog that makes work outside the walls difficult and dangerous. Though it originates from multiple areas of the forest at once, the fog cannot cross or exist within 5 feet of fresh water (magical or otherwise), and as such cannot cross the river surrounding Pinewood. Elizabeth knows from experience that even 10 minutes in the fog can cause terrible headaches or worse. This is why harvesting Bloom Pollen is a very rare event.
The Annual Festival
The annual festival draws trade from all over the region, transforming the entire village into a thriving marketplace that swells with exotic and mysterious visitors and wares.  The festival always starts on the eve of the first Full Moon after harvest.  Mirigad’s Marvelous Circus, is a travelling circus that is currently established in Pinewood for the festival. Aldric and Aidan are well known in town for they bring an extra supply of wine and spirits out of the Verdant Outpost for the event.
The Underdark Entrance
The entrance is remembered as the Moon Gate, a shifting threshold said to open only when moonlight and fog align, then vanish again before dawn. Only one Warden has ever seen it, but given the Grey Fog, they did not know how to find it again. The Covenant still teaches that it exists, and Elizabeth believes the gate is real, but its true location remains unknown. Too many people and beasts have disappeared in the area.
Common Lore on Lycanthropes
Once, there were many. Too many, some would say. But people are afraid of what they cannot control, and hunger - especially the hunger for flesh - is not so easily tamed. It is the same story, told again and again across history. Whispers of a beast in the night. Silver coins pressed into windowsills. A family disappearing. Then, a mob with torches and blades. They drove the werefolk from these lands, deeper and deeper, until there was nowhere left to run but down to The Underdark. And once something is swallowed by the deep, well… it seldom returns the same. If at all. I suspect they either perished or became something else entirely.
Lycan Combat Notes
Lycanthropic Regeneration. A lycanthrope of sufficient might (Elite lycan monsters, 11 Level PCs) will regenerate 1 Hit Die at the start of its turn if (1) it is below half its hit point maximum and (2) has not taken radiant damage since the end of its last turn. A lycanthrope PC must have a Hit Die available to expend.
Silvered Weapons. Silvered weapons deal an additional 1d6 radiant damage to lycanthropes.
Full Moons. Lycans automatically transform into Hybrid Form on Full Moons. Those in Covenant are usually able to suppress this transformation, but it’s not guaranteed. (It will require a CON save)
