# Tirrimal

## PC Notes

Aasimar Bard (Dance / College of Dance)

**Why Aasimar Dance Bard?** 
- You were never a mortal at all.
- Born from the Weave itself; Physical manifestation or fragment of the Weave, given temporary sentience.
- Your soul floated through the Weave, learning its rhythms. You are in harmony with the vibrations of the Weave (not actual performative dance).
- Learning to behave as a person. Their new body was meant to be more magical and less physical; they feel drawn to a mystical kind of being.
- A profound, almost uncomfortable intimacy with magic itself. You feel the weave physically. Almost "as one" with the Weave (hence arcana expertise)
- Can feel the Weave's vibrations in objects and others (this was the reason for most of my other skill choices and spells)

**How did you come to be**
When Oona the Lost fell and the Sporion was broken, a shock wave was felt throughout the corrupted ley lines in the swamp.  Something changed. In the days that followed, the corruption transformed. It spread outward through the Weave like ink in water. Slow. Patient. Certain.

Threatened more than ever, the Weave's natural defenses answered by manifesting **Tirrimal**! Deep in the depths of the Underdark, where a ley line still ran clean, arose an otherworldly humanoid. That was two weeks ago. Since then, the naive Tirrimal has been climbing. Climbing up towards the world above. Climb up towards a sustained wrong note in the Weave. Tirrimal knows not what this pain is, but they're certain they must find it. And destroy it.

**Reflavoring of Aasimar & College of Dance Bard skills**
- Tirrimal will not actually be a traditional bard with physical dancing.
- *With spells*, they're likely going to be striking their bodhran (rhythm-maker's drum) and using those vibrations to focus the weave into a physical manifestation, directing the vibrations with their hands.
- *Their inspiration* will likely be transferred via foot stomping, possibly a pat or elbow to the ribs when close.
- *Their footwork skills* will be sort of like a compulsion.
- <something else> will replace *Necrotic Shroud* a be frightening (without wings)
- <something else> will replace *Heavenly Wings* and provide flight (without wings)

### Motivations & Desires

- I really like the idea of self-discovery along the way and a struggle within the character as to their eventual return to the weave or autonomy.  It would be interesting to explore this along the way.

### Backstory

When Oona the Lost fell and the Sporion was broken, a shock wave was felt throughout the corrupted ley lines in the swamp.  Something changed. In the days that followed, the corruption transformed. It spread outward through the Weave like ink in water. Slow. Patient. Certain. Threatened more than ever, the Weave's natural defenses answered by manifesting an otherworldly humanoid.

Deep in the depths of the Underdark, where a ley line still ran clean,  Tirrimal arose!

That was two weeks ago. Since then, Tirrimal has been climbing. Climbing up towards the world above. Climb up towards a sustained wrong note in the Weave. Tirrimal knows not what this pain is, but they're certain they must find it. And destroy it.

### Key Moments & Plot Hooks

- **Ley lines / Venom** as the secret engine: DM can foreshadow through Weave-flavored transformations or migraines near corrupted nexuses.

- Later arcs (**Underdark**, **Feywild**) as places the Weave “sounds different”—opportunity for memory shards or opposing pulls (balance vs. fey caprice).

- **Sporion seed husk** (whatever the party carries from the Sporion): Tirrimal senses it through the Weave—a sustained wrong note in the network. It works like an unconscious **compass**, tugging attention and path toward the object even if they do not know what it is.

- **Compulsion**: Their mandate to restore balance manifests as a drive to **destroy** the husk. Play this as instinct, intrusive urge, or moral certainty - tension if the party means to study, keep, or weaponize it.

### Goals

- Restore **balance** to the Weave / ley network.
- Create their own **identity** and self-will.
